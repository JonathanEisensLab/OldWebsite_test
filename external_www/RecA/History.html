For a good review of the isolation of the first recA mutant in E. coli, see Clark AJ, 1996 (recA mutants of E. coli K12: a personal turning point. Bioessays 18, 767-772).

  • 1965: The first recA mutant was isolated by Clark and Margulies
  • 1967: Witkin reported that lambda induction by UV and UV induced filamentations had many parallels
  • 1973: M. Radman suggested that DNA damage generated a signal that led to the induction of a set of lexA, recA regulated genes.
  • 1976: recA gene of E. coli cloned by McEntee
  • 1979: Roberts et al showed than RecA protein had protease activity
  • 1980: Sancar et al. reported the sequence of the E. coli recA gene
  • 1980: Kenyon and Walker used lacZ in Mu phage to identify SOS induced gene regions.
  • 1984 :Little showed that the LexA and lambda repressors can autodigest at pH 9 or 10 without RecA
  • 1992: Story, Weber, and Steitz reported the crystal structure of the E. coli RecA protein monomer/polymer and a lower resolution structure of RecA bound to ADP
  • 1992: Aboussekhra et al. and Basile et al. report the determination of the sequence of the yeast Rad51 and show it encodes a protein similar to RecA
