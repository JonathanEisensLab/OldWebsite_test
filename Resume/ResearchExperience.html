Research Experience

  • Ph.D Research, Stanford University, June 1991 - current.
      Research in the lab of Prof. Philip C. Hanawalt. Currently studying DNA repair in the extremely halophilic Archaea Haloferax volcanii and the evolution of DNA repair proteins, in particular RecA and proteins in the SNF2 family.
  • Research Assistant, Harvard University, September 1989 - March 1991
      Undergraduate research and (after graduation) employment as a research assistant in the lab of Prof. Colleen M. Cavanaugh. Applying molecular techniques to characterize the chemoautotrophic sulfur-utilizing endosymbionts of the marine invertebrate Solemya velum (Bivalvia:Mollusca).
  • Research Assistant, Harvard University, May 1989 - December 1989
      Undergraduate research and summer employment in the lab of Prof. Fakhri A. Bazzaz. Helped design and conduct study on the effects of variation in light and water quality and quantity on the physiology of four species of birch (Betula). Also conducted independent experiment on circadian rhythms in Abutilon theophrasti.
  • Field Assistant, Rocky Mountain Biological Lab, Summer 1988
      Internship with Prof. William A. Calder III, University of Arizona. Monitored weight and temperature rhythms, dive energetics, foraging strategies, nesting success, and behavior of alpine hummingbirds.
